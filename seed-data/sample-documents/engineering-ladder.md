# Engineering Ladder - From Pup to Alpha

> *In an otter raft, every member grows from playful pup to skilled hunter. Here's our engineering growth framework.*

## Levels

### Pup (L1) - Junior Engineer
**Scope**: Individual tasks within a single service

- Delivers well-defined tasks with guidance
- Writes clean, tested code that follows existing patterns
- Participates in code reviews and learns from feedback
- Asks questions proactively and documents learnings
- Completes onboarding checklist within first 2 weeks

**Example milestone**: Ships a bug fix or small feature end-to-end with minimal hand-holding.

### Yearling (L2) - Engineer
**Scope**: Features within a service or small cross-service changes

- Independently delivers features from spec to deployment
- Writes comprehensive tests (unit, integration)
- Provides constructive code review feedback
- Contributes to technical discussions and ADRs
- Participates in on-call rotation

**Example milestone**: Owns a feature from RFC to production, including monitoring and documentation.

### Adult (L3) - Senior Engineer
**Scope**: Leads technical direction within a squad

- Breaks down ambiguous problems into clear technical plans
- Mentors Pups and Yearlings through pairing and review
- Authors RFCs and ADRs for significant technical decisions
- Identifies and addresses tech debt proactively
- Leads incident response and writes postmortems
- Influences squad roadmap priorities

**Example milestone**: Authors and implements an RFC that improves a key system metric (latency, reliability, cost).

### Elder (L4) - Staff Engineer
**Scope**: Cross-team technical leadership

- Sets technical direction across multiple services
- Resolves the hardest cross-cutting technical challenges
- Establishes engineering standards and best practices
- Represents engineering in strategic planning
- Builds tools and frameworks that multiply team productivity
- Mentors Senior Engineers toward Staff track

**Example milestone**: Drives a multi-quarter initiative (like Project Riverbank) that measurably improves platform reliability.

### Alpha (L5) - Principal Engineer
**Scope**: Organization-wide technical strategy

- Defines the long-term technical vision for OtterWorks
- Makes decisions that shape the architecture for years
- Partners with CTO on build-vs-buy-vs-open-source decisions
- Represents OtterWorks in the broader engineering community
- Unblocks the entire organization through technical leadership

**Example milestone**: Architects a system redesign that enables the next order-of-magnitude growth.

## Promotion Criteria

Promotions are based on **sustained demonstration** at the next level, not tenure. You should be consistently operating at the next level for 3-6 months before formal promotion.

| Signal | Weight |
|--------|--------|
| Technical impact | 40% |
| Collaboration & mentorship | 25% |
| Scope & ownership | 20% |
| Communication & documentation | 15% |

## Review Cadence

- **Weekly 1:1s** with your manager
- **Quarterly growth check-ins** with level assessment
- **Annual promotion cycles** (April and October)
- **Peer feedback** collected quarterly via 360 reviews

*Remember: the goal isn't to climb the ladder fastest -- it's to grow into the otter the team needs you to be.*
